History of Matcha

Matcha is a type of green tea that originates from Japan and has been an important part of Japanese culture and traditions for centuries. The history of matcha begins in the 12th century when green tea was brought to Japan from China by the Japanese Zen monk, Eisai. Eisai introduced green tea and the method of grinding tea leaves into a fine powder, which later became the basis for making matcha. Matcha became increasingly popular during the Edo period (1603-1868), where drinking matcha became part of the famous Japanese tea ceremony.

Facts About Matcha

Matcha has a higher caffeine content than other green teas because we consume the entire tea leaf when drinking it.
Matcha is rich in antioxidants, especially catechins, which can help fight free radicals and improve health.
Matcha is used in various dishes and drinks, such as matcha lattes, matcha ice cream, cakes, and others.
Apart from being used as a drink, matcha is also used in meditation, art and spiritual practices in Japan.
